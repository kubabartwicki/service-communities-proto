Learn about the phases of a community

We use the phases below to assess the maturity of a given community. These phases will help you assess where your community is and what you should consider next.

  • {{ govukTag({ text: "discovery" }) }}

    Communities which are in the discovery phase are relatively new and have held 1-3 workshops. In this phase communities should:

    • Define the scope of the service area
    • Identify relevant departments, agencies and third parties
    • Give people an understanding of what the community might achieve
    • Understand how the community will work together to achieve that goal
    • Start creating a common understanding of who provides what for the user

  • {{ govukTag({ text: "alpha" }) }}

    Communities which are in the alpha phase are established communities and have held 3 or more workshops. In this phase communities should:

    • Finish creating a common understanding of who provides what for the user, with all services (guidance, transactions, support) mapped
    • Make users and community participants aware of what steps users need to do with government to achieve their goal
    • Give everyone an understanding of how the community will work together to achieve that goal

  • {{ govukTag({ text: "beta" }) }}

    Communities which are in the beta phase are recognised communities and have successfully delivered against their community objectives. In this phase communities should:

    • Create a common understanding of what user needs are and where they aren't being met
    • Prevent users needing to repeatedly enter information across different transactions
    • Improve the transactions within the service
    • Save taxpayers money through reducing duplication
    • Create a common understanding of what the community needs to deliver next
    • Create a GOV.UK step by step guide

  • {{ govukTag({ text: "live" }) }}

    Communities which are in the live phase are mature communities and are continuously improving their respective service area. In this phase communities should:

    • Create teams funded by more than one organisation to work together on a problem
    • Track and measure improvements to the service
